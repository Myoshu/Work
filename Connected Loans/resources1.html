Starting your home buying journey

Buying your first home is a big decision which is filled with excitement and happiness. Here at Connected Loans we are here to support you through this process. To get you started we have put together out initial tips to get the get ball rolling so you can buy your own place.

Create a Budget

Saving for a home loans deposit is the first step in the home buying process. Utilise a budget planner so you can calculate how much you can save each month without having to sacrifice life style. It’s a good idea to use high interest savings account and term deposits to ensure your money working hard for you.

Create a Plan and Set Goals

Use our calculators so you know how you can borrow on how much deposit you will need to save. Once you know this information it’s important to set yourself goals and to ensure you have a plan in place so you achieve your goals.
The bigger the deposit the smaller the loan you will need. If you manage to save 20% deposit this will mean you avoid Lenders Mortgage Insurance which is another cost. As a minimum you will need to put towards 5% of the purchase price, pay for all associated fees and additional costs (like removalist!)

Understand the costs

Use our calculators so you know the costs of buying a home early in the process. Knowing these costs will ensure that your savings goals will mean that you have saved enough money to pay for your deposit and all the costs.
Understanding the costs of buying a home can help you avoid unexpected bills and allows you to work out how much you’ll need to save as a deposit for your home.
Here is some of the most common home buying costs, in addition to the purchase price of the property:                                              
Stamp Duty: this is a State Government tax which is calculated on the purchase price of the property. This is usually paid before to settlement. Your solicitor or conveyancer will help facilitate payment.
Building and pest inspections: these should be once you are ready to buy a property but before contracts are exchanged. The reports will ensure the property is structurally sound and free of termites and other pests
Registration fees: these are paid to the Land Titles Office in your state or territory when you submit any documentation relating to your property
Lenders’ Mortgage Insurance: this one-off charge is normally applicable if you borrow more than 80% of the value of the property.
Insurance: you should consider the needs for building insurance.
Legal costs: your solicitor or conveyancer should be able to provide you with an itemised quote which will include the transfer of title, property and title searches and contract reviews
Removalist costs and Utilities.Moving into new house will usually moving all of your belongings and it’s important to make sure you factor removalists. Don’t forget too when you move you’ll need to disconnect and reconnect your utilities too.

What is a pre-approved loan?

A pre-approval is also known as a conditional approval, an indicative approval, approval in principle, AIP or Homeseeker.
There are different types of pre-approval available in the market today and vary from lender to lender. A pre-approval is only an indication from a lender that will offer you finance. A Pre-Approval is not an offer for finance and in some instances will not have been assessed by the departments that approve loans. There is still a risk that the lender may decline your application upon completing a full assessment.
In today’s environment it’s very common for people to obtain a pre-approval before looking for a property to buy. To overcome the risk that a lender may not decline with your application it’s recommended to find a lender that will assess your loan and issue you a pre-approval subject to finding an acceptable property. This means the lender will have accepted your financial situation and will have verified your income, credit history and deposit source.
Connected Loan can help you find that right approval so you can look to buy property knowing that your finance is in the best position possible.

What does a solicitor/Conveyancer do?

Conveyancing is the process of transferring ownership of a legal title of land (property) from one person or entity to another – this process will done by a licenced conveyancer or a registered solicitor.
Your conveyancer or solicitor will help with the settlement of your new property by looking after your rights and meeting all your legal obligations. Some of things they will do are

  • Prepare, clarify and lodge legal documents associated with your new purchase. Examples of these documents are the contract of sale and memorandum of transfer.
  • Investigate the property and its certificate of title – check for easements, irregularities and investigate any issues.
  • They will arrange for the deposit to be held in trust account. The deposit paid will held in trust until settlement by the vendor’s representative.
  • They will calculate the payment of any rates and taxes
  • They will facilitate settle the property with the vendor. They will act on your behalf, advise you when the property is ready for settled, contact your bank or financial institution to book settlement and attend settlement when final payments are made

The Home Buying Process

Making an offer is important part of the home buying process. Whilst it may seem complicated once you become familiar with steps involved in the process you will much more confident in successfully negotiating and buying a home.

How do I make an offer?

You should initially review the Contract of Sale provided by the vendor or their real estate agent. The contract of sale will include information about the property that will influence your decision to buy. Your solicitor or conveyancer can help interpret information in the contract of sale.
Once you reviewed the contract and you want to proceed you will need to make an offer. Make your offer in writing and include any conditions attached with your offer. Again your solicitor or conveyancer can help with making the offer.
You most likely move into the negotiation phase with the vendor and real estate agent. If a price is agreed you will then need to exchange contracts and pay the deposit. To exchange contract you will need to sign the contract of sale with the vendor. Your solicitor or conveyancer, mortgage broker and lender can help with this part of the process.

What is cooling off?

In most states there is usually a cooling-off period which applies to after contracts are exchanged. During the cooling-off period you are allowed to cancel the contract but you depending on the circumstances you may lose your deposit. It’s best to talk with solicitor or conveyancer about a cooling-off period and if it will apply to your contract.

What is a property auction?

An auction is a gathering of potential buyers who will publically bid for a property that want to buy. It is usually conducted by an auctioneer, whom will acting on behalf of the vendor and real estateme agents. The auctioneer and real estate agent are governed by strict rules.
This type of buying of property is very different to other property purchases. As the bidding process is public, if the property meets the reserve or the price, the hammer falls and if you are the highest bidder, you have to immediately sign the contract.
Understand the following auctions terminology:
Reserve price – before the auction commences, the vendor is required to set a price with the auctioneer that is the minimum price they will accept for the property. If the reserve price isn’t met during the auction, the auctioneer will ask the vendor if they will sell at a lower price and reduce the reserve price.
Passed in – if the biddingprocess do not meet the reserve price, the auctioneer will continue to search for more bids. If bids still do not meet the reserve, the property will have been 'passed in' or ‘withdrawn from auction’. The person that is the highest bidder then gets first opportunity to negotiate with the seller.
Fall of the hammer – if a property meets the reserve price the auctioneer will announce that reserve price has been met.  Once the bidding has finalised and there are no more bids, the auctioneer will do what is known as the ‘fall of the hammer’, which will signal the end of the auction. No further bids can be made and the highest bidder is legally obliged to sign and exchange contracts. 

Settlement

What are my loan documents?

Once you have successfully found your home you will need to finalise your approval. The lender will verify your financial position (unless already completed) and verify that the property is suitable then issue an unconditional approval.
Each lender has different process for preparing loan documents but either they will prepare and issue you with your loan document or instruct a panel solicitor to do this on their behalf.
You will then receive a loan agreement pack that will have the required documents for you to read and sign so your loan can proceed to settlement. In loan agreement pack you will receive a loan agreement or loan offer. This will include details such as the loan amount, the interest rate and the repayments. Included with documents there will the Lender’s Terms and Conditions. This can be the form of a booklet or a document.
There will also be additional forms such as a Mortgage document. This document allows the lender to register their mortgage on your property for security purposes.
You may also receive a Direct Debit form that is completed so the lender has authority to collect repayments from your bank account.
You can go through the documents by yourself, ask for help from a Connected Loans specialist or seek advice independent legal advice from a solicitor. At Connected Loans the choice is yours.

Do I need property insurance?

Lenders will usually require that any property they take for security has adequate building insurance before settling the loan. Typically their value will provide advice on the recommended insurance amount and they will also require that the listed as an interested party.
Depending on your property type the insurance will vary:
House: You will need building insurance and provide the lender will a copy of your policy
Unit/townhouse/villa: The Strata committee organise building insurance and can produce the policy is required.
Vacant Land: as there are no properties on vacant land building insurance is not required.

How do I get my FHOG?

The first home owners grant (or FHOG) is a one-off payment to first home owners that meet eligibility criteria. It was set up to help first home buyers to enter the property market. The grants are funded by either the Federal or Statement Government however they are managed by the State Governments.
The actual grant amount varies between each state and territories with some State Governments offering concessions or exemptions on stamp duty.
Most Lenders will be registered agents to act on behalf of the State Government and assist with the processing of the FHOG applications so that the monies and concessions are applied before or at settlement.
As the eligibility criteria, grants and stamp duty concessions vary across the country its best to talk with Connected Loan home loans specialists so we can help you with any FHOG enquires you may have.

Is Property investment right for you?

Borrowing to invest is an investment property has risks; therefore, it’s very important that investors understand allrisks and costsassociated with investment properties and weigh these against the benefits. Unless you an experienced investor you should seek advice from an independent professional to ensure that your strategy is the right strategy for you.
If you are looking to use this strategy ask yourself these questions:

  • Do you have secure income or cash reserves from other sources such as savings to top up the loan if you don’t have a tenant or the tenant stops paying their rent?
  • Do you earn a high income you can make the most of any tax benefits?
  • Are you looking for short term or long term gains? Due the costs and low liquidity property investment is generally a medium to long term strategy (at least 5 to 10 years).
  • Do you have any flexibility with your investment strategy to allow for changes in your personal circumstances such as starting a family or if you income changes?
  • If your investment isn’t performing will you become anxious and lose sleep at night ?

Tips for Choosing the Right property

Where to buy

Familiar markets - Consider buying your property investment in an area you are already familiar with.  Areas you are familiar with should take you less time to research. Its important to check recent sale prices of houses in the area to give you an idea of what you can expect to pay for your investment property.
Growth suburbs - Look in locations where high growth is expected. Potential for capital gains is key element of property investing. Look for areas with infrastructure projects like new transport system, schools or Government funded projects.
Rental yield - Look in locations where you can achieve good rental yield.Yield is the rental income compared to the property value.
Low vacancy rates - Find out about the vacancy rates in the location. If there are high vacancy rates then it may suggest the area is less then desirable. You might have an extended period looking for a tenant.

What to buy

Attractive features - Look for properties with features that will appeal renters. Looks for things such asmore than 1 bathroom, a lock up garage or that is close to shops, schools and transport.
Wide appeal - Look for a property that will appeal to singles, couples, young families or retirees. The more people your property appeals the easier it will be to find a tenant.
Low maintenance - Some older homes or those that have a pool or extensive landscaping may cost more to maintain.It’s important to try and keep maintenance costs down.

Essential property investment Strategies

Equity

Many property investors will use equity from their home to help buy their 1stinvestment property.  They then use the equity from both their home and investment property to buy their next property. As people accrue equity this allows owning a portfolio of investment properties easier over time.
An important component of equity is to understand how not to over-extend yourself. If you max out your equity position it will leave you vulnerable financially as you have no buffer to use in an emergency.

Depreciation

The Government offer generous tax breaks; particular around depreciation—a good idea is to ensure your investment property is mostly paid for by the tenant and any tax savings you will get. Engage a professional quantity surveyor to give you a depreciation schedule. This maximise your potential tax deductionsand savings.

Negative gearing and positive cash flow

It’s important that you understand how negative gearing works. Many first up investors will use negative gearing start investing in property. To negative you must however be able to contribute funds each month towards the costs of your property. Over time, the property rental income will improve so you can then achieve a positive cash flow and you won’t have to contribute additional funds.

Investment property research

Always important to do your research on where you are going to buy. Always look to buy in sought-out locations, close to public transport with access to decent schools and amenities. This means you should find good tenants without difficulty.
You can buy anywhere in Australia and it’s a good idea to diversify your portfolio – this will reduce concentration risk. Buying in different locations will help protect your portfolio in case something adverse happens in the location of your investment property.

Loan Types Explained

What is Standard /Basic Homeloan

Standard / Basic home loans are a type of loan offers a low ongoing interest rate, with very little ongoing fees and often a reduced or waived application fee, however, as a trade-off these loans offer very little flexibility and have nominal features such as on offset account, ATM cards, cheque books and BPAY.
These loans are suited to everyone and its best to talk to a Connected Loans home loans specialist so we can find the right loan for you.

What is an offset homeloan

An offset account is a regular savings account that typically has a debit card, internet banking and a cheque book. Offset accounts work differently in that instead of earning interest of the balance of savings account that amount is deducted from your mortgage when calculating its interest payable.
Mortgages generally have a higherinterest rate then savings account which means that you benefit from further savings.
An additional benefit on offset account relates to interest deductions that are allowed on investment loans. If you make extra repayments on an investment loan and then later access that money via redraw you most likely will not be to claim the interest on that portion of the loan. The ATO will look at the purpose of the funds used for redraw to see if they are for investment purposes.
An offset account overcomes this issue as you will never actually be paying down your home loan. The offset account is also a good idea if you ever want to convert on owner occupied property into an investment property. By using an offset account you will maximise your interest deductions which will assist in your investment strategy.

What is an Equity Loan

An Equity loan is where you get a loan using the equity in your property. Equity in simple terms is the difference between your loan balance and what the value of your property.
Equity Loans are offered by many lenders and come in the form of a Line of Credit.
A Line of Credit is where you have a loan where you can access the money at settlement or at separate stageslater – this makes this type of loan very useful for people wanting to do renovations or investing who need access to various amounts of money at different times.

What is a Line Of Credit

A line of credit (LOC) mortgage is a type of loan that allows you to access your loan in full at settlements or at a later date. You can use the money up to your credit limit as you require and you can access the money in as many stages as you like.
The payment options all vary from lender to lender. Some lenders will still require monthly payments and other will accept no payments being made as long as you stay below the credit limit.
A line of credit can be used personal use; personal investment and depending on the lender business use will also be allowed. Most people use a line of credit to complete the purchase of new properties, home renovations and for buying shares.
To establish if a line of credit is suitable for you call a Connected Loan specialist today on 03 8696 6300 or Enquire online.

What is a Fixed Rate Loan

A fixed rate loan is when your interest rate is guaranteed during the fix rate period. This means your monthly repayment will remain the same during the fix rate period.
Fix rate loans do offer certainty around what your repayments will be but also lack the flexibility that a variable loan has. Some lenders charge a fee for fixing your loan, will limit what additional repayments can be made and if your repay your loan early (i.e you sell your home and discharge your loan) you could be subject to a penalty.
To establish if a fixed loan is suitable for you call a Connected Loan specialist today on 02 8696 6300 or Enquire online.

What is a Alt Doc or Low Doc Loan

Alt Doc or Low Doc is a flexible solution loan available to self-employed people. Its home loan product designed for people who are unable to provide their latest tax returns and financial statements. These products rely on an income declaration form where a self-employed states their income which is then verified through an assessment of other documents like bank statements, business activity statements and letters from accountants.
Lenders have different lending criteria for these loans so to establish if a Alt Doc / Low Doc loans is suitable for you call a Connected Loan specialist today on 02 8696 6300 or Enquire online.

Typical Homeloans features

Interest only repayments

Interest only repayments are feature that is available on some home loan types. Interest only loans allow you to make repayments of the interest only and no principle repayment is required. These loans are very popular for when the loan is used for investment purposes.
This loan feature isn’t suitable for everyone or available on every home loans so to know more information about interest only repayments call a Connected Loan specialist today on 02 8696 6300 or Enquire online.

Redraw facility

A home loan with a feature called a redraw facility allows you to withdraw money from your mortgage when you have made extra repayments. These facilities typically offer you flexible payment terms where you can increase your minimum repayments or make lump sum payments.
If you have spare money this is a good place to put it so you can save interest on your home loan. There are something to watch out for like minimum redraw amount, how you can do redraw and fees for a doing a redraw.

Repayment holiday

A home loan repayment holiday is when you are able to take a break from making your regular mortgage repayments. Depending on your circumstances your lender will allow this for pre-decided time period. It’s for helpful for people whom need to free up cash flow due to planned or unplanned life events.
Lender approval is needed, who may allow for a period of up to 12 months – its important noting each lender criteria is different and that you will in most instances will have had to of made extra  repayments to cover the repayments needed during your repayment holiday.

Professional Packages

Many lenders will offer professional package for loans over $250,000 but some lenders will allow then with loans as little as $100,000. Professional Packages usually bundle up numerous features with your home loan and includes things like;

  • Credit cards with no annual fee
  • Free or discounted offset and savings accounts
  • Interest rate discounts on variable rate home loans
  • Discount on other products such as building, income protection and landlord insurance.
  • Fee waivers or reductions on valuations, top-ups and switches

The products may not be as competitive as a Basis/Standard loan but they too offer some appealing features that will suitable to some people.
To get more information if Professional Package loan is suitable for you call a Connected Loan specialist today on 02 8696 6300 or Enquire online.

Portable Loans

Portable loan or loan portability is a feature offered on loans which will allow you to keep your loan when selling your property and buying a new one. This will save you the time with having to arrange a new loan and the costs of discharging your current loan (this is great if you have a fixed loan!) and paying for a new loan. This feature will be subject to lender criteria which can include things like you are unable increase the loan amount and you will need to settle the your sale and new purchase on the same day.

Loan Splitting

Loan splitting is when you divide your loan into multiple accounts. The benefit of splitting your loan allows you to have portion on different interest rate types.
By way of example if you split your loan 50/50 you can have half your loan on a variable rate and the other half on a fixed rate.
